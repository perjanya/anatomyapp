Celiac trunk

Celiac trunk

Introduction

  • Artery of foregut
  • Anterior branch of abdominal aorta

Development

 

The celiac trunk appears in the fifth week of embryonic development at the level of the seventh cervical segment

  • Final adult position - at the lower third of the first lumbar vertebra

Relation to median arcuate ligament

Passes in front of the aorta at the level of the first lumbar vertebra, usually just above the origin of the celiac trunk

Relation to celiac ganglia

The left and right celiac ganglia lie on either side of the base of the celiac trunk, concealing its origin from the aorta

Branches

  1. Hepatic artery
  2. Splenic artery
  3. Left gastric artery (smallest branch)

Largest branch

  • In fetus –hepatic artery
  • In adult – splenic artery

Celiac artery compression syndrome:

Mostly by the median arcuate ligament
