Prechordal plate

  • It is a transient, thickened region of the cranial epiblast in the bilaminar embryonic disc.
  • Serves as the rostral organizer for head and forebrain development.
  • It precedes the notochord and plays a pivotal role in gastrulation
  • It establishes the anterior-posterior (A-P) axis through SHH-dependent inhibition of BMP/Wnt signaling,
  • Ensures proper telencephalon and eye field formation.

Prechordal plate cells are pioneer ingressor cells via RhoA/ROCK cytoskeleton, with Eph/ephrin guiding rostral path

RhoA/ROCK signaling pathway is essential for the apical constriction and force generation required for individual cells to ingress through the primitive streak/node, a process that involves epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT).
