Development of GIT

Lateral plate mesoderm: somatopleuric → body wall; splanchnopleuric → future gut.

Splanchnopleuric layers fuse → mesogastrium (double fold suspending gut tube).

Ventral mesogastrium: gut to ventral body wall (limited to foregut region; absent caudally → single peritoneal cavity).

Dorsal mesogastrium: gut to dorsal body wall (persists as most mesenteries: dorsal mesentery proper, transverse mesocolon, small intestine mesentery, sigmoid mesocolon).

GIT lining epithelium: endoderm-derived

Parts of the Gut (Based on Arterial Supply & Boundaries)

Foregut:

  • Buccopharyngeal membrane to major duodenal papilla (hepatopancreatic ampulla).
  • Cranial: pharynx, larynx
  • Caudal: esophagus, stomach, proximal duodenum (D1 + upper D2), liver, gallbladder, pancreas.

Midgut:

  • Major duodenal papilla to proximal 2/3 transverse colon.
  • Distal D2, D3/D4, jejunum, ileum, cecum, appendix, ascending colon, proximal 2/3 transverse colon.

Hindgut:

  • Distal 1/3 transverse colon to cloacal membrane.
  • Distal transverse colon, descending colon, sigmoid, rectum, upper anal canal (to dentate line).
