Week 3: Gastrulation and Trilaminar Disc Formation

Key Timeline and Events

  • Day 15 (Gastrulation Onset):
    • Primitive streak forms caudally in epiblast (BMP4/Wnt signaling from extraembryonic tissues)
    • Epiblast cells ingress as mesendoderm precursors
    • Bilaminar disc elongates to pear-shaped (~1 mm).
  • Days 16-17 (Streak Elongation and Notochord):
    • Streak extends cranially
    • Primitive node (cranial end) organizes notochord process (midline mesoderm)
    • Prechordal plate thickens cranially (head organizer)
    • Caudal eminence forms at streak tail.
  • Days 18-19 (Trilaminar Disc Establishment):
    • Ingression displaces hypoblast â†’ definitive endoderm
    • Lateral plate mesoderm splits into somatic and visceral layers
    • Neural plate induces from ectoderm (notochord SHH signals).
  • Days 20-21 (End of Week 3):
    • Streak regresses
    • Somites begin forming from paraxial mesoderm (segmentation clock via Notch or Delta).
    • Oropharyngeal membrane (buccopharyngeal) and cloacal membrane appear as ectoderm-endoderm junctions
    • Allantois buds from caudal endoderm

Key structures formed

Primitive Streak: Transient groove in epiblast (caudal to rostral). Site of gastrulation ingress. Primitive node (thickened cranial end) â†’ notochord.

Trilaminar Germ Disc:

Ectoderm: Neural plate (dorsal, future CNS/PNS); surface ectoderm (epidermis).

Mesoderm: Paraxial (somites: vertebrae/muscles), intermediate (kidneys/gonads), lateral plate (somatic: body wall; visceral: gut serosa).

Endoderm: Definitive (gut lining, respiratory); yolk sac remnants.

Notochord: Rod-like midline structure from Hensen's node; induces neural tube, defines body axis.

Prechordal Plate: Cranial thickened epiblast/mesendoderm; secretes SHH for forebrain induction.

Membranes: Buccopharyngeal (cranial, future mouth); cloacal (caudal, future anus/urogenital); allantois (vascular outpouching for umbilical cord precursor).
