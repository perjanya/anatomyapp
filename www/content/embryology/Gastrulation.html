Gastrulation

Definition

Gastrulation is a series of events that result in the formation of the 3 germ layers namely ectoderm, endoderm and mesoderm.

Mechanism

Collective movement of cells through the primitive streak and to interior of the embryo to form the three primary germ layers constitutes gastrulation

All the three germ layers are derived from the epiblasts.

Process

Week 3 (days 15-21): Epiblast-derived ingress through primitive streak (caudal, day 15).

Non-migrating epiblast → ectoderm (neural or surface).

Ingressing cells:

  • Cranial (primitive node → notochord or axial mesoderm)
  • Intermediate (paraxial/intermediate mesoderm)
  • Caudal (lateral plate).

Definitive endoderm from mesendoderm displaces hypoblast.

The third week of intra uterine life is also marked by the formation of the notochord.

Gastrulation commences with the formation of Primitive streak.

Gastrulation insights explain 40-50% of major congenital anomalies and early embryonic lethality

Clinical relevance

Neural Tube Defects (NTDs): Notochord induction failure (SHH disruption) → spina bifida (1:1,000); folic acid (0.4mg) reduces risk 70% by aiding methylation.

Situs Inversus: Nodal asymmetry from streak defects; ciliopathies (e.g., Kartagener) cause 50% heterotaxy.

Caudal Regression Syndrome: Incomplete streak regression (diabetes teratogen) → sirenomelia; maternal glycemic control prevents 80% cases.

IVF Embryo Viability: Gastrulation arrest in poor-quality blastocysts

Gastrulation Organoids: Model mesoderm induction for drug testing (e.g., valproate NTDs)

Early Pregnancy Loss: ~30% from layer misspecification; serial hCG + USG (week 5) detects, with progesterone rescuing 15%.

Regenerative Medicine: EMT mimics for stem cell differentiation
