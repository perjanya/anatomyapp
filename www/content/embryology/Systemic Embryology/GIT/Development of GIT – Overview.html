The enteric nervous system (ENS), sometimes called the 'second brain', is entirely derived from vagal and sacral neural crest cells.

ENS development requires RET signaling (via GDNF) and EDNRB signaling (via endothelin-3). Failure of these pathways underlies Hirschsprung disease.

Molecular Basis — Foregut Patterning

• SOX2 expression: specifies esophageal/respiratory fate in cranial foregut

• NKX2.1 (TTF-1): master regulator of lung and thyroid from foregut endoderm

• PDX1 (IPF1): essential transcription factor for pancreatic development;

PDX1 mutations → pancreatic agenesis or MODY4

• HNF4α and FOXA2: liver specification from foregut endoderm

• Notch signaling: controls hepatocyte vs. biliary fate decision

• FGF from cardiac mesoderm + BMP4 from septum transversum → hepatic specification

• RA (retinoic acid) gradient: regulates foregut-midgut boundary positioning

  • Midgut (superior mesenteric artery):
    • Duodenum distal to major duodenal papilla (lower D2, D3, D4).
    • Jejunum, ileum.
    • Cecum, appendix, ascending colon, proximal 2/3 transverse colon.

🧬 Molecular Basis — Midgut

• CDX2: master regulator of intestinal identity; expressed throughout midgut & hindgut endoderm

• Wnt/β-catenin pathway: essential for gut elongation and intestinal crypt-villus axis formation

• FOXA1/2, GATA4/5/6: regulate intestinal differentiation

• Midgut rotation driven by asymmetric expression of LEFTY2 and NODAL on the left side (left-right axis)

• SHH (Sonic Hedgehog) from endoderm → mesoderm signaling for smooth muscle and connective tissue development

• Physiological herniation is normal — failure to return = exomphalos (omphalocele)

  • Hindgut (inferior mesenteric artery):
    • Distal 1/3 transverse colon.
    • Descending colon, sigmoid colon, rectum.
    • /3 anal canal (to dentate line).

🧬 Molecular Basis — Hindgut & Cloaca Division

• BMP4 and SHH from hindgut endoderm regulate urorectal septum growth

• HOXA13 and HOXD13: mutations cause hand-foot-genital syndrome and cloacal anomalies

• WNT5A: critical for urorectal septation; knockout mice develop persistent cloaca

• FGF10/FGFR2 signaling: controls cloacal membrane integrity

• RET/GDNF axis: neural crest migration; mutations → Hirschsprung (can affect sigmoid/rectum)

• SHH → Gli2/Gli3 in mesoderm → patterns anorectal region

Hindgut

Inferior mesenteric artery (IMA)

The arterial supply reflects the embryological segmentation. This is clinically important: 'watershed zones' at foregut-midgut junction (splenic flexure) and midgut-hindgut junction are vulnerable to ischemia during hypotension or after aortic surgery
