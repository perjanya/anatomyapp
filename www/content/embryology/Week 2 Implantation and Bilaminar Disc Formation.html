Week 2: Implantation and Bilaminar Disc Formation

Key Timeline and Events

  • Day 7 (Early Implantation):
    • Hatched blastocyst apposes to endometrium (fundus wall, posterior preferred)
    • Trophectoderm adheres via integrins/L-selectins to uterine selectins.
    • Syncytiotrophoblast invades decidua, forming lacunae for maternal blood pooling (start of hemochorial placentation).
  • Days 8-9 (Deep Implantation):
    • Syncytiotrophoblast erodes spiral arteries (MMPs/secreted proteases)
    • Cytotrophoblast proliferates as stem cells
    • Embryonic pole embeds ~0.2 mm
    • Abembryonic pole remains superficial
    • hCG detectable in maternal serum (~5 mIU/mL).
  • Days 10-12 (Bilaminar Disc Emergence):
    • Embryoblast (inner cell mass) flattens into bilaminar disc (~0.1-0.2 mm oval/pear-shaped).
    • Epiblast (dorsal, columnar) â†’ embryo body; hypoblast (ventral, cuboidal) â†’ extraembryonic endoderm.
    • Cavities form: Amniotic (epiblast-derived, small) and primary yolk sac (hypoblast + exocoelomic membrane).
  • Days 13-14 (End of Week 2):
    • Uterine epithelium regenerates over abembryonic pole (covering lacunae)
    • Lacunar networks connect to maternal sinusoids (primitive circulation).
    • Decidua capsularis forms around conceptus
    • Histiotrophic nutrition peaks via gland secretions (glycoproteins, lipids).

Key Structures Formed

  • Trophoblast Differentiation: Syncytiotrophoblast and Cytotrophoblast
  • Bilaminar Disc: Epiblast (future ectoderm/mesoderm/endoderm via gastrulation) and hypoblast (yolk sac endoderm).
  • Adhesions at cranial (prechordal plate precursor) and caudal (cloacal membrane) ends.
  • Extraembryonic Membranes:
    • Amniotic cavity (roof: amnioblasts from epiblast)
    • Yolk sac (floor: hypoblast, nutrient role until week 8)
    • Chorion (trophoblast + extraembryonic mesoderm, future placenta).
  • Decidua:
    • Progesterone and Leukemia inhibitory factor (LIF) induced stromal changes
    • Natural Killer cells (uNK cells) of uterus remodel arteries for low-resistance flow
