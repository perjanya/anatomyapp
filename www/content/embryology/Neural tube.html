Neural tube

Development of CNS begins in the 3rd week of IUL.

Neural plate

It is a pear-shaped thickening of ectoderm.

It overlies the notochord and paraxial mesoderm.

Neural folds

These appear due to folding of neural tube along longitudinal axis.

Neural groove

Longitudinal depression enclosed by the neural folds.

Neural tube

It is formed with the fusion of neural folds. It presents cranial and caudal neural pores. These neural pores get closed to form a tube by the 5th week.

Differentiation of neural tube & its developmental components

Neural tube differentiates into:

  • Caudal: Cylindrical → spinal cord (central canal).
  • Cranial: Bulbous → brain vesicles (week 4 primary).

Primary Vesicles

  1. Prosencephalon (Forebrain): Telencephalon (cerebral hemispheres, lateral ventricles) + diencephalon (thalamus, III ventricle).
  2. Mesencephalon (Midbrain): Cerebral aqueduct.
  3. Rhombencephalon (Hindbrain): Metencephalon (pons/cerebellum) + myelencephalon (medulla); dilates to IV ventricle.

Cavities of neural tube

Cavities of neural tube show changes as:

Spinal cord - remains unchanged - central canal

Rhombencephalon - dilates - IVth ventricle.

Mesencephalon - remains unchanged - cerebral aqueduct

Prosencephalon :

Diencephalon - III ventricle.

Telencephalon - enlarges - lateral ventricle.

Histogenesis of neural tube

From within outwards three layers are formed -

  1. Ependymal
  2. Mantle
  3. Marginal

Neural tube defects:

  • Failure of the neural tube to close results in defects such as spina bifida, meningocoele and anencephaly defects
  • Spina Bifida/Myelomeningocele: Posterior neuropore failure
  • Anencephaly: Anterior closure defect (lethal, 1:10,000); AFP screening (week 16) + termination counselling
  • Hydrocephalus: Aqueduct/ventricle malformation (e.g., Dandy-Walker from rhombencephalon); endoscopic ventriculostomy
  • Chiari malformation: Herniation from hindbrain overcrowding (myelencephalon); MRI biomarkers (week 20) predict
  • Teratogen prevention: Valproate disrupts mantle layer → autism or NTDs (3x risk)
  • Regenerative CNS: Ependymal stem cells for spinal repair
