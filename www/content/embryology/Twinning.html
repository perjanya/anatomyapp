Twinning

Harnessing two or more foetuses simultaneously is called twinning.

Classified as

Dizygotic (fraternal): two zygotes from separate ova/sperm)

Monozygotic (identical): one zygote splitting

Dizygotic twins

  • Double ovulation/fertilization, FSH-driven
  • Two blastocysts implant separately (day 7-10)
  • Always dichorionic-diamniotic
  • Two independent embryonic discs, chorions, amnions
  • Fused placentas possible but separate villi

Monozygotic twins

  • Early split (days 1-3, morula): dichorionic-diamniotic (70%, separate placentas and amnia).

  • Mid split (days 4-8, blastocyst): Monochorionic-diamniotic (25%, shared placenta and separate amnia).

  • Late split (days 9-13, post-implantation): Monochorionic-monoamniotic (1-2%, shared everything, high-risk).

  • Very late (post-week 13): Conjoined (1:50,000-200,000 MZ, incomplete split)
