Oogenesis

The formation, development, and maturation of an ovum

Primordial germ cells (PGCs)

PGCs appear in yolk sac endoderm (week 3-4 IUL)

Migrate via hindgut dorsal mesentery to gonadal ridge by week 6 IUL.

In XX embryo, genital ridge → ovary develops by week 8.

Oogonia

Oogonia (from PGC mitoses) peak at ~7 million (week 20)

Enter meiosis I → primary oocytes by birth (~1-2 million, 80% lost via atresia)

Primary oocyte

Primary oocytes enter diplotene arrest mid-gestation; resume briefly pre-ovulation

Arrest at Diplotene by ~week 18 (mid-gestation), sustained via NAT1/NPM2 factors

Meiotic arrest - Diplotene stage of prophase I

Follicular Development Stages

Primordial follicle

Primary oocyte + single layer flattened pre-granulosa

Maturation (monthly cohort post-puberty, FSH-driven)

Oocyte enlarges; flattened → cuboidal granulosa;

Zona pellucida forms between oocyte-granulosa)

Secondary follicle

Multilayer granulosa. Theca differentiate into externa and interna

Antral or Graafian

Antrum forms; oocyte eccentric with cumulus oophorus; protrudes at ovarian surface

Secondary oocyte

  • LH surge: Primary oocyte completes meiosis I
  • Forms secondary oocyte (haploid, 23X) + 1st polar body.
  • Arrests at metaphase II (Mos kinase stabilization).
  • Fertilization: Ca²⁺-dependent calpain degrades Mos → meiosis II finishes

Number of primordial follicles

  • Highest - Not at birth
  • Peaks at 5th month - 7 million
  • Most degenerate
  • 70000 - 2 million at birth
  • 40000 at puberty

Primordial follicles = Continuous follicular/oocyte degeneration (apoptosis)

Regulation

Fetal AMH (Sertoli/granulosa homolog) → Müllerian regression in males.

Pubertal: GnRH → FSH (follicle recruitment) + LH (theca/ovulation)

Oogenesis: Asymmetric, finite process producing one viable ovum per cycle; begins in utero, completes post-fertilization.

Clinical relevance

Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI): Accelerated atresia/depletion of primordial pool (e.g., genetic FMR1 mutations) causes early menopause

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): Disrupted FSH/LH → follicular arrest at antral stage (no dominance); metformin + clomiphene targets recruitment for ovulation induction

Turner Syndrome (45,X): Failed PGC migration → streak gonads (no oocytes); hormone replacement + fertility preservation (oocyte donation) informed by fetal ridge defects

Fertilization Failures: Mos degradation defects (rare mutations) prevent meiosis II completion → polyspermy/aneuploidy; Ca²⁺ ionophores in ICSI mimic sperm-induced release.

Cancer Therapy Protection: Alkylating chemo targets dividing oogonia/follicles; GnRH agonists suppress recruitment during treatment to preserve pool.
