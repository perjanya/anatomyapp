Implantation

Definition

Process of sequential apposition, adhesion, and invasion of blastocyst into the uterine endometrium via trophoblast is referred as implantation.

Sequence: apposition → adhesion → invasion

Site and time of implantation

Common site of implantation is posterior wall of uterine fundus.

Time: day 6-10 post-fertilization

Fundus posterior preferred, but anterior equally viable in IVF

The uterus at the time of implantation will be in the secretory phase

hCG maintains corpus luteum and menstrual cycle ceases on successful implantation.

Uterine endometrium

The endometrium, now known as the decidua in pregnancy, thickens to form a suitable nidus for the conceptus

Decidua

  • Progesterone-induced stromal hypertrophy or edema of uterine endometrium.
  • Forms nidus with spiral artery remodeling
  • Types
    • Capsular (at site of implantation)
    • Basal (under chorion)
    • Parietalis (rest of the uterine endometrium)

Confirmation of implantation

hCG Monitoring: Syncytiotrophoblast secreted hCG rise (doubling q48h) confirms implantation; subnormal in vanishing twin or molar (cytotrophoblast overgrowth)

Trophoblast lineage

  1. Syncytiotrophoblast
    1. a multinucleated mass of cytoplasm
    2. immunologically inert, via FasL
    3. Invasive – erodes endometrium
    4. Produces hCG (human Chorionic Gonadotropin)
  2. Cytotrophoblast
    1. Inner cuboidal cells
    2. Represent a germinative population of Trophoblast cells
    3. Renews syncytiotrophoblasts

Molecular Regulators

  • LIF/HOXA10 for receptivity
  • MMPs/integrins for invasion
  • hCG sustains luteal phase

Ectopic sites of implantation

The fertilized oocyte may get implanted anywhere along the path of its journey to the uterus. Implantation outside the uterine cavity is called ectopic pregnancy.

These abnormal sites are:

  1. Fallopian tube (tubal pregnancy) – most common site of ectopic gestation (~95%)
  2. On the ovary (ovarian pregnancy, ~3%)
  3. In the peritoneal cavity and other orgrans (abdominal pregnancy, <1%)
  4. Cervix of uterus (cervical pregnancy)

Window of Implantation (WOI)

  • The WOI is the optimal 4-5 day period (~days 20-24 of a 28-day menstrual cycle) when the endometrium is receptive to blastocyst implantation
  • It is driven by progesterone-induced decidualization and expression of adhesion molecules (e.g., integrins, LIF).

Outside this window, the endometrium is either proliferative (pre-ovulatory, non-receptive) or refractory (post-WOI, leading to menses), explaining ~20-30% of implantation failures in IVF

Clinical relevance

  • Ectopic pregnancy management
    • Tubal ectopics (95%) risk rupture (day 6-8 invasion mimics)
    • β-hCG + USG detects early
  • Placental Abnormalities
    • Excessive syncytiotrophoblast invasion → accreta spectrum
    • C-section history + USG Doppler screens, with hysterectomy prevention via interstitial laser
  • Endometriosis Impact
    • Impaired adhesion causes 40% infertility
    • pre-IVF ablation + progesterone enhances window of implantation

Endometrial Receptivity Array (ERA)

Definition and Purpose

ERA is a molecular diagnostic test used in IVF to assess endometrial receptivity by analyzing the expression of 238-248 genes from an endometrial biopsy, identifying the personalized window of implantation (WOI) for optimal embryo transfer timing

Procedure

Performed via a simple office biopsy (similar to a Pap smear) on cycle day ~21 (or adjusted), with RNA extracted and profiled; results take 4-6 weeks, guiding a subsequent frozen embryo transfer cycle.

Indications

Primarily for women with recurrent implantation failure (RIF) or unexplained infertility, as ~25% have a displaced WOI (e.g., 12-24 hours earlier/later than standard), potentially improving success rates by 20-30% in select cases
