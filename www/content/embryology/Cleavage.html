Cleavage

Definition

Cleavage is a series of rapid, successive mitoses of zygote producing smaller blastomeres

(e.g., 2-cell day 1, 4-cell day 1.5, 8-cell day 2).

Divisions asynchronous initially, without cell growth.

Later the process becomes more synchronous by reaching morula stage.

After 8-cell stage (third division), compaction via E-cadherin junctions forms polarized cells: Inner (future ICM) vs. outer (future trophectoderm).

Cleavage divisions lack G1, S, and G2 phases

In humans cleavage is holoblastic (involves complete division of the entire zygote)

Stages: Zygote ‚Üí 2-4-8 cells (cleavage) ‚Üí compaction (8-cell) ‚Üí morula (16-cell) ‚Üí blastocyst (day 5)

Differentiation

  1. Inner cell mass
    1. E-cadherin-mediated adhesions polarize blastomeres, positioning inner cells (with apical-basal asymmetry) to form the inner cell mass (ICM).
    2. ICM gives rise to the embryo proper and some extraembryonic tissues.
    3. Inner cells have active Hippo (YAP cytoplasmic ‚Üí ICM fate)
  2. Trophectoderm
    1. In contrast, outer cells adopt a free surface, excluding Hippo pathway effectors like YAP from the nucleus, directing them toward trophectoderm fate for placental development
    2. Outer cells have inactive Hippo (YAP nuclear ‚Üí trophectoderm fate)

Blastocyst

  • Fluid accumulation between trophectoderm cells creates the blastocoel
  • Blastocoel= a fluid-filled cavity that defines the blastocyst stage by day 4-5 post-fertilization.
  • "Blastocyst" = trophectoderm + ICM + blastocoel
  • The fluid of blastocoel originates from maternal sources in the uterine cavity, diffusing through tight junctions in the trophectoderm, and is actively pumped into the intercellular space by Na+/K+-ATPase pumps on the apical surfaces of trophectoderm cells.
  • Aquaporin-8 channels facilitate water influx following this osmotic gradient.

Asynchronous divisions allow variable cell fates, enhancing totipotency

Regulation

Oct4/Nanog maintain totipotency

Hippo pathway polarizes outer cells (YAP/TAZ nuclear exclusion)

Clinical relevance

  1. IVF Embryo Grading: Day 3 cleavage stage (6-8 symmetric cells) predicts blastocyst potential; poor compaction/asynchrony flags aneuploidy, guiding PGT-A biopsy.
  2. Recurrent Implantation Failure: Trophectoderm defects (e.g., Hippo pathway mutations) prevent attachment
  3. Assisted Hatching: Laser thinning of zona pellucida aids day 5 hatching in older patients, boosting implantation.
  4. Early Miscarriage Risk: Blastomere fragmentation (>20%) from oxidative stress ‚Üí biochemical pregnancy
  5. Time-Lapse Monitoring: AI algorithms track cleavage kinetics; predicts euploidy, cutting multiple births in single-embryo transfer.
