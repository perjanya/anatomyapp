Rotator cuff muscles

Formed from tendons of supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor and subscapularis

Tendons of these four muscles fuse with underlying capsule

Primary function of rotator cuff is to hold the large head of humerus with a shallow glenoid cavity.

It allows fine adjustments of head of humerus within the glenoid cavity

Applied aspects:

Dislocation

  1. Shoulder joint is more vulnerable for dislocation than any other joint in the body
    • Anterior dislocation is more common than posterior dislocation
    • Fall on the outstretched hand with shoulder abducted and externally rotated dislocates shoulder anteriorly.
  2. Recurrent anterior shoulder dislocation – abnormal anatomical factors responsible
    • Hill-Sach’s lesion – posterolateral lesion in head of humerus
    • Bankart’s lesion – anterior part of glenoid labrum
    • Erosion of anterior rim of glenoid cavity

Frozen shoulder

Definition – painful restriction of both active and passive shoulder movements due to causes within the shoulder joint or remote

Causes of frozen shoulder are tendinitis of rotator cuff, bicipital tendinitis, fractures and dislocations around shoulder.

Rotator cuff lesions – are usually associated with supraspinatus tendon.
