Deltoid

It’s an inverted delta shaped ‘∆’ muscle on the upper outer part of arm

Origin:

  • Lateral 1/3rd of clavicle – anterior unipennate
  • Acromion process – middle multipinnate
  • Crest of spine of scapula – posterior unipennate

Insertion:

  • V- shaped deltoid tubercle/tuberosity

Nerve supply:

  • Axillary nerve (C5 and C6)

Action:

  • Middle fibres – abduct the arm
  • Anterior fibres – flex and medially rotate the arm
  • Posterior fibres – extensors and laterally rotate the arm

Intramuscular injections are given to deltoid

Needle is inserted vertically - Four finger breadth below the acromial process or into the upper part of lower half of deltoid

Axillary nerve is at risk of injury

Clinical testing

  • Ask the patient to abduct the arm against resistance

‘At risk’ structures undercover of the deltoid

  • Axillary nerve anterior branch with posterior circumflex humeral artery

Axillary nerve supplies deltoid and teres minor
