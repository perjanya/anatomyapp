Humerus

Etymology: humorous – may be secondary to tingling sensation after ulnar nerve is stimulated

Upper end

  • Features: head, neck, greater and lesser tubercle with intertubercular sulcus
  • Head is covered with articular cartilage and articulates with glenoid cavity and intervening labrum to form glenohumeral joint
  • Constriction just next to head is anatomical head – glenohumeral joint capsule is attached here
  • Junction between diaphysis and epiphysis represented by epiphyseal line is morphological head
  • Short constriction in upper end below the greater and lesser tubercle is surgical neck

[IMP]Surgical neck of humerus is related to axillary nerve with posterior and anterior circumflex humeral vessels

Anatomical predisposition to supracondylar fractures

  1. Supracondylar region is the junction between diaphysis and metaphysis
  2. This is the region of transformation of compact bone to cancellous bone
  3. The shape of humerus changes from circular profile to triangular profile below this level
  4. Below this region – humerus exhibits medial and lateral supracondylar ridges. This essentially divides the power of bone into two pillars
  5. During the fall on outstretched hand the line of force passes through this region posteriorly.

Mechanism of injury – fall on outstretched hand with elbow hyperextended, arm abducted/adducted and wrist hyperextension
