Clavipectoral fascia

  • Fills the space between pectoralis minor and clavicle

Extent

    • From clavicle to axillary fascia below
    • In the upper part, it slits to enclose the subclavius and adjacent clavicle. Posterior lamina continues as investing layer of deep cervical fascia
    • In the middle it splits and enclose the pectoralis minor muscle
    • In the lower part it blends with axillary fascia
    • In the lower part it is called suspensory ligament of axilla
    • Medially it blends with external intercostal membrane of upper two intercostal spaces
    • Laterally it is attached to coracoid process and blends with coracoclavicular ligament
    • Thick upper part of fascia extending from the first rib near costochondral junction to coracoid process is called costoclavicular ligament

Structures piercing clavipectoral fascia

    • Lateral pectoral nerve
    • Thoraco-acromial artery
    • Lymphatics
    • Cephalic vein
