Axillary artery

Artery of axilla

Continuation of subclavian artery

Continues as brachial artery

Extent: from outer border of first rib to lower border of teres major

Along with axillary vein it is very closely related to cords of brachial plexus

Relations

First part relations

  • Lateral cord and posterior cord are lateral to the artery
  • Medial cord is posterior

Second part

Second part relations

  • Lateral cord is lateral
  • Medial cord is medial
  • Posterior cord is posterior

Third part

Third part relations

  • Medial - Median root of median nerve, medial cutaneous nerves of arm and forearm
  • Lateral - lateral root of median nerve and musculocutaneous nerve
  • Posterior - Radial nerve and ulnar nerve

Applied aspects

  • Axillary pulse can be felt against humerus
  • Anuerysm of axillary artery
    • Presents with pulsatile swelling in axilla
    • Compresses branches of brachial plexus
  • Through cervicoaxillary canal â€“ infections of spine can spread to axilla
  • In case of obstructed distal part of subclavian artery or proximal part of axillary artery â€“ blood can reach distal upper limb via scapular anastomoses

Axillary vein

Axillary vein extent

Formation - union of two brachial veins and basilic vein

Continues as - subclavian vein beyond outer border of first rib

Axillary vein â€“ tributaries

Cephalic vein - after passing through deltopectoral groove, cephalic vein pierces clavipectoral fascia and enters axillary vein

Other tributaries correspond to branches of axillary artery
