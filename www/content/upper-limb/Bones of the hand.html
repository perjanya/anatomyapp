Bones of the hand

Carpal bones

8 carpal bones

Proximal row from radial to ulnar side

Scaphoid, Lunate, triquetrum and pisiform

Distal row from radial to ulnar side

Trapezium, Trapezoid, Capitulum and Hamate

Peculiarities of pisiform

It is a sesamoid bone in tendon of flexor carpi ulnaris

It ossifies by 10-11 years

Peculiarities of Scaphoid bone and scaphoid bone fracture

It is a boat shaped proximal row radial side carpal bone

Fracture scaphoid is the most common carpal bone fracture

Arterial flow to the scaphoid enters via the distal pole and travels to the proximal pole

Avascular necrosis is the most common complication of scaphoid bone fracture
