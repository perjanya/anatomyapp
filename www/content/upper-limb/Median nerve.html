Median nerve

  • Also called labourers nerve, ‘peripheral eye’
  • “Median” in position in forearm
  • Supplies most flexors of forearm and thumb muscles
  • Median nerve innervates - abductor pollicis brevis and opponens pollicis muscles in hand. Contraction of these muscles gives humans the ability to oppose the thumb joint
  • Injury to both ulnar and median nerves results in total claw hand
  • Root: C5,6,7,8 and T1

Formation

Union of medial root (branch of medial cord) with lateral root (branch of lateral cord of brachial plexus) – ‘Y’ shaped union

Anterior Interosseous Nerve

  • Arises from lateral side of median nerve distal to pronator teres

Innervates

  • Flexor pollicis longus
  • Pronator quadratus
  • Lateral half of flexor digitorum profundus forming digital tendons for index and middle fingers

It ends by supplying distal radioulnar, radiocarpal and carpal articulations

Flexor pollicis brevis

The flexor pollicis brevis has two heads a superficial and a deep.

The superficial is innervated by the median nerve

The deep is innervated by the ulnar nerve

Cutaneous innervation by Median nerve

Autonomous zones of median nerve

Applied aspects - Tests for detecting median nerve injury

  • Pen test- patient is unable to touch the pen held at a distance in horizontal plane, due to the loss of action of abductor pollcis brevis
  • Pointing index or Oschner’s clasp test: index and middle finger fail to flex during clasping with both hands. This is due to loss of action of long flexors going to these fingers
  • Benediction test: patient is unable to flex the fore finger and middle finger on raising the hand
