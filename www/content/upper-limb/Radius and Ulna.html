Radius and Ulna

Ulna

Ulna is the medial bone of the forearm. It is the postaxial bone of the upperlimb

Long axis is in line with humerus

It has upper end, shaft and lower end (head)

Upper end

Olecranon - curved eminence in the upper end, receives insertion of triceps 

Coronoid process - triangular eminence project forwards, receives insertion of brachialis 

Semilunar notch - articular space between olecranon and coronoid process 

Radial notch - narrow depression on the lateral side of olecranon, articulates with radius (lateral bone)

Body (shaft)

Borders - anterior, posterior and lateral border 

Lateral border is sharp - interosseous border 

Surface - anterior, posterior and medial surface 

Generally flexors of the wrist and digits arise from the anterior surface 

All extensors arise fom the posterior surface 

Lower end (head of ulna)

Articulates with the upper surface of the triangular articular disc

Ulnar styloid process - directed laterally, non-articular projection of the lower end

Radius

Radius is the lateral bone of the forearm. It is the preaxial bone of the upperlimb

Weight is transmitted through radius (from hand) and interosseous membrane to ulna, in turn to humerus

Parts of radius - upper end, shaft and lower end

Upper end

Head of the radius - forms joint (humeroradial - hinge) with capitulum of the humerus

Neck - located below the head, this is the usual level of division of brachial artery into radial artery and ulnar artery

Radial tuberosity - located on the medial side. Biceps brachii is inserted into its posterior aspect, separated by a bursa (related to anterior aspect of radial tuberosity)

Shaft

Borders - anterior, posterior and medial border (interosseous border)

Surface - anterior, posterior and lateral surfaces

Generally, flexors of the wrist and digits arise from the anterior surface 

All extensors arise fom the posterior surface 

Lower end

Large and quadrilateral, has anterior, posterior, medial, lateral and inferior surfaces 

Radial artery is palpated over the distal and lateral aspect of the anterior surface of the radius

Inferior surface articulates with the carpal bones (scaphoid and lunate) to form radiocarpal (wrist) joint
