Scapula

  • Also called shoulder blade
  • It has a body and spinous process, acromion process and coracoid process
  • Coracoid process
    • Etymology: coraco – beak; oid-like
    • Tip of coracoid process can be palpated 2.5 cm below the junction of lateral 1/4th and medial 3/4th of clavicle
    • Attachments – muscles
      • Tip of coracoid process – short head and biceps and coracobrachialis (origin)
      • Base – insertion of pectoralis minor
    • Attachments – ligaments

Coracoclavicular ligament, coracoarcomial and coracohumeral ligaments

Represents atavistic epiphysis

Pseudofracture of lateral border is due to enlarged circumflex scapular artery eroding the lateral border as happens in collateral circulation in obstructed axillary artery
