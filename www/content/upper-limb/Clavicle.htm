Clavicle

Peculiarities of clavicle

·        It is the only long bone placed horizontally

·        It has no medullary cavity

·        It is the first bone to ossify in intrauterine life (mandible ossifies second)

·        It is the only long bone having two secondary centres

·        It is the only long bone ossifying from intramembranous ossification

·        It is pierced  by many cutaneous nerves

Anatomical factors that are of significance in fracture clavicle

·        Clavicle fractures at junction of medial 2/3rd and lateral 1/3rd

o   As it is the junction of natural curvature of clavicle

o   Transmission of forces from clavicle to scapula occur at this site through coracoclavicular ligament

·        Lateral segment is displaced inferiorly by weight of the limb and drawn medially by teres major

[CLINICAL] Anatomic complications of fracture clavicle

·        Lateral segment is displaced downwards due to pull of teres major muscle under gravity with weight of the limb. Medial segment is pulled up due to the action of sternocleidomastoid.

·        Neurovasular injury – commonly injured are subclavian vessels and medial cord of brachial plexus
