Movements of thumb and other fingers

Opposition of the Thumb

Opposition depends primarily on function of the intrinsic muscles of the thumb, especially the abductor pollicis brevis

Opposition of the thumb is a complex motion made by coordination of

  1. Abduction of the thumb from the palmar surface of the index finger
  2. Flexion of the metacarpophalangeal joint of the thumb
  3. Internal rotation or pronation of the thumb
  4. Radial deviation of the proximal phalanx of the thumb on the metacarpal
  5. Motion of the thumb toward the fingers

Most important single muscle that initiates opposition of thumb is abductor pollicis brevis

Abductor pollicis brevis

  • Rotates thumb internally
  • Abducts the thumb away from the index metacarpal
  • Abducts the proximal phalanx of the thumb on its metacarpal
  • Assists the extensor pollicis longus in extending the interphalangeal joint of the thumb

Restoration of opposition

Opposition is either partially or totally lost in poliomyelitis or median nerve palsy.

In restoring opposition by tendon transfer, the transferred tendon is inserted into the tendon of the abductor pollicis brevis.
