Midpalmar space

Boundaries:

  • Superficially: palmar aponeurosis, superficial palmar arch, flexor tendons of medial three fingers covered with common synovial sheath (ulnar bursa) and medial three lumbrical muscles
  • Deep: deep fascia covering interossei and metacarpal bones (3rd to 5th)
  • Medially (ulnar): medial palmar septum
  • Laterally (radial): midpalmar septum

Thenar space

Boundaries:

  • Superficially: palmar aponeurosis, superficial palmar arch, flexor pollicis longus with flexor sheath (radial bursa) and first lumbrical muscle
  • Deep: deep fascia covering adductor pollicis
  • Medially (ulnar): midpalmar septum
  • Laterally (radial): lateral palmar septum

Midplamar space and thenar spaces extend distally upto the distal palmar crease and beyond this they communicate with lumbrical canals. If there is collection of pus in midpalmar or thenar space (pus tanks), it can be drained via lumbrical canals (that acts as pus taps)

Midpalmar space + thenar space = pus tanks

Lumbrical canals = pus taps, through web spaces

    • Extends from heads of metacarpal bones to distal phalanx
    • Consists of flexor tendons

Tenosynovitis

Web space

  • Soft tissue between the bases of the fingers
  • Distally - skin and natatory ligament
  • Posteriorly - deep transverse metacarpal ligament
  • Proximally - deep attachments of the palmar fascia, together with their lateral attachments to the tendon sheaths

Deep palmar space

  • Complex three-dimensional space limited proximally by the carpal tunnel
  • Lies deep to the palmar aponeurosis, between the radial and ulnar condensations of vertical fibres which connect the palmar aponeurosis to the thenar and hypothenar eminences
  • Partitions that pass deeply from the longitudinal bands of the palmar aponeurosis form eight narrow compartments: four contain the digital flexor tendons and four contain the lumbricals and the neurovascular bundles

Pulp spaces

  • Confined compartments bounded by the digital creases which overlie the joints
  • Attached to the underlying pulleys

Forearm space of Parona

  • Boundaries
    • Deep: pronator quadratus;
    • Superficial: flexor carpi ulnaris, flexor digitorum profundus, median nerve, flexor pollicis longus

Infection in the hand

Effects of infection of hand can be devastating as it leads to severe pain and loss of functionality.

Pus can get collected in the deep palmar spaces â€“ thenar or midpalmar space

Pus in these two spaces can be tapped via lubrical canals by passing into the web space

Pus can get accumulated in the finger

Paronychia â€“ is infection of eponychium

Digital pulp space infection (or Felon, Whitlow) â€“ is the infection of digital pulp space. Since it is a tight compartment, pus accumulation can cause severe pain. Infection can penetrate deep up to the bone.
