Gross anatomy of pancreas

Etymology

Pancrease - Pan = all; kreas = flesh

All fleshy gland in the abdomen - pancreas

Functions

Endocrine part: Produces insulin, glucagon, somatostatins and pancreatic polypeptide

Exocrine part: digestive juice rich in bicardonate and enzymes like proteases, trypsinogen, chymotrypsinogen, lipase, amylase, phospholipase A2, lysophospholipase and cholesterol esterase

Structure of pancreas – Parts

  • Head of pancreas
    • Lies within the concavity of duodenum
    • From its lower aspect Uncinate process emerge

Neck of pancreas

    • 1cm long
    • Lies between head and body
    • Behind superior mesenteric artery

Body of pancreas

    • Largest part of pancreas
    • Present at transpyloric plane behind the pylorus of stoach

Tail of pancreas

    • Present near the spleen hilum  

Uncinate process

    • Hook like projection from the head and body of the pancreas
    • Superior mesenteric vessels are anterior to this

Margins – borders

Upper margin

Splenic artery runs along this border

Lower margin

Super mesenteric vessels emerge from this margin

Anterior margin

Two layers of transverse mesocolon is attached

Surfaces

Inferior surface

Covered by peritoneum, lies over duodenojejunal flexure and coils of jejunum

Anterior surface

Related to lesser sac and stomach

Omental tuber - prominence on the neck of the pancreas

Posterior surface

Devoid of peritoneum

In contact with aorta and origin of superior mesenteric artery, splenic vein, left kidney and left renal vessels, left suprarenal gland

Relationship with superior mesenteric vessels

Superior mesenteric artery

Anterior to uncinate process

Superior mesenteric vein and formation of portal vein

Posterior to the neck of pancreas margin

Blood supply

Branches of celiac trunk and superior mesenteric artery

Splenic artery runs along this border

Splenic artery

Supply body, neck and tail

Run along the upper border

Splenic artery is tortuous - Not a universal phenomenon. 

Probable reasons - to accommodate for the movement of spleen during respiration and expansion of the stomach

Superior and inferior pancreaticoduodenal arteries

Related to head of pancreas

Duct system

Major pancreatic duct - Duct of Wirsung

Joins common bile duct before piercing the duodenal wall.

Sphincter of Oddi guards these ducts

Minor pancreatic duct - Duct of Santorini

Not a common feature in all

Can either drain into duodenum (70%) or drain into the main pancreatic duct (30%)

Development

From two sources

Dorsal pancreatic bud - larger

Forms anterior part of head, neck, body and tail of pancreas

Its duct forms the main portion of main pancreatic duct

Ventral pancreatic bud - smaller

Forms the posterior part of head and uncinate process

Its duct forms the minor pancreatic duct  

Lymphatic drainage

Follow arteries and drain to

  • Pancreaticosplenic group
  • Coeliac group
  • Superior mesenteric nodes
  • Pyloric group of lymph nodes

Applied aspects

Diabetes mellites

Caused by insulin resistance and relative deficiency of insulin secretion by islets of Langerhans

Carcinoma head of pancreas

Compress the common bile duct - results in obstructive jaundice

Chronic pancreatitis

Chronic inflammation of pancreas - most commonly in alcoholic individuals

Pancreatic cysts

Saclike fluid collection in pancreas -surgery of which necessitates thorough knowledge of stomach bed structures.
