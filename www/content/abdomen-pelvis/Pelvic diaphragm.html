Pelvic diaphragm

Introduction

Floor of the true pelvis

Funnel shaped fibromuscular partition in the pelvis

Components of pelvic diaphragm

  • Levator ani muscles
  • Coccygeus muscles
  • Superior and inferior fascia enclosing these muscles

Superior fascia of pelvic diaphragm

Anteriorly - To the body of the pubis and to superior rami of pubis

Laterally - Over the obturator fascia

Posteriorly - With the piriformis fascia and with anterior sacrococcygeal ligament

Inferior layer of pelvic diaphragm

Laterally - Continuous with obturator fascia

Blends with fascia on the external anal sphincter

Openings in pelvic diaphragm

Hiatus urogenitalis

Opening between the right and left levator ani muscles

Contains - urethra in males and vagina with urethra in females

Hiatus anorectalis

Opening between the perineal body and anococcygeal raphe

Contains – anorectum

Levator ani

Broad, thin muscle at the pelvic floor

Critical role in pelvic support

Components

  • Pubococcygeus
    • Levator prostate in males and
    • Pubovaginalis in females
  • Puborectalis
  • Iliococcygeus

Pubococcygeus

Origin

From the inner surface of the body of pelvis

From the tendinous arch of obturator fascia

Insertion

Fibres from pubis are inserted into the perineal body

Fibres from the tendinous arch are inserted into the anococcygeal raphe and to the coccyx

Subdivisions

Anteriorly

In males - fibres around the prostate forms - levator prostate

In females - fibres around the vagina and cervix form pubovaginalis

Pubovaginalis - acts as vaginal sphincter

Posteriorly

U shaped sling around the rectum forms puborectalis

Puborectalis blends with the longitudinal fibres of the rectum

Puborectalis - creates anorectal angle. It is one of the important factors for the anal continence

Iliococcygeus

Origin:

From the posterior part of the tindinous arch

From the ischial spine (pelvic surface)

Insertion: into the anococcygeal raphe and to the sides of the coccyx

Ischiococcygeus

Origin: apex of ischial spine and sacrospinous ligament

Insertion: coccyx and to the S5 segment of sacrum

Sacrospious ligament - considered as degenerated tendon of the coccygeus

Kegel exercises

Voluntary contractions of all the perineal muscles

Strengthen all the striated muscles in the perineum

Uses -

  • Ameliorate erectile dysfunction due to venous leakage
  • Help men control premature ejaculation
  • Treat urinary incontinence in females

Role of pelvic diaphragm during labour

During 2nd stage of labour, pelvic diaphragm - guides the fetal head descent

Functions of pelvic diaphragm

  • Supports the pelvic viscera.
  • Elevates prostate.
  • Sphincter of vagina.
  • Coccygeus pulls coccyx forwards.
  • Puborectal sling relaxes to assist defaecation.
  • Puborectal sling assists rotation of fetal head.
  • Relaxation of pubococcygeus helps in micturition by permitting descent of bladder neck.

Nerve supply

On the pelvic surface - direct branches of ventral rami of S4

On the perineal surface - branches of perineal nerve and inferior rectal nerve

Ischiococcygeus - by branches of S3 and S4

Clinical significance - Levator ani syndrome

Due to intermittent, episodic spasm of the levator ani muscle, patient may experience sharp buring pain or tenesmus of the rectal and/or perineal area

Hiatus of Schwalbe

A gap between the tendinous origin of levator ani & obturator fascia is called Hiatus of Schwalbe.

Pelvic organs may herniate through this gap causing ISCHIORECTAL HERNIAS.
