Portal system

System of veins connecting two capillary networks is portal system

The portal vein

  • Connects intestinal capillaries with hepatic sinusoids
  • Provides a mechanism to deliver the absorbed contents from the GIT to liver, before blood enters systemic circulation
  • Transports degraded products of senescent RBCs to liver
  • Constitutes 80% of blood flow to liver
  • Tributaries may contain upto 1/3rd of entire circulatory volume

Formation

  • Union of superior mesenteric vein with splenic vein
  • Behind the neck of pancreas

Course

  • Runs behind neck of pancreas
  • Posterior to first part of duodenum
  • In free margin of lesser omentum (gastroduodenal ligament)
  • Ends in porta hepatis
  • Divides into right and left branches

Parts and relations

  • Infraduodenal part – below D1
    • Anterior – neck of pancreas
    • Posterior – IVC
  • Duodenal part  - behind D1
    • Anterior – D1, Bile duct and Gastroduodenal artery
    • Posterior – IVC
  • Supraduodenal part – above D1
    • Anterior – hepatic artery and bile duct
    • Posterior – IVC

Tributaries

  • Splenic vein – receives inferior mesenteric vein
  • Superior mesenteric vein –
  • Superior pancreaticoduodenal vein
  • Left and right gastric vein
    • Right gastric vein – receives prepyloric vein of Mayo (landmark for identification of pyloric sphincter during surgery)
  • Cystic vein
  • Paraumbilical veins
