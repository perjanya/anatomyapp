Portocaval anastomoses

Definition

Communication between portal system of vein with somatic veins is portocaval anastomosis

Purpose

  • They provide alternative channels for the venous return from the GIT in case of portal vein block or hepatic sinusoidal obstruction as happens in cirrhosis liver
  • In such cases these enlarged channels manifest as different clinical conditions.

Sites of portocaval anastomoses

Portal hypertension: Increased pressure within the portal system

Pressures: Normal pressure : 1-4 mmHg. Below 6 mmHg

Pressure more than 6 mm Hg - Portal hypertension

Important causes

Within the liver

Liver cirrhosis

Outside the liver

Structural changes in portal vein and obstruction

Wedged hepatic venous pressure (WHVP)

Determined by wedging a catheter in a hepatic vein

Occlude the hepatic vein

Measure the pressure of proximal static blood

Hepatic venous pressure gradient (HVPG)

Clinical measurement of the pressure gradient between the WHVP and the free hepatic venous pressures

Estimate of the pressure gradient between the portal vein and the inferior vena cava

An HVPG of ≥5 mmHg defines portal hypertension

Measurement exceeds 10 mmHg it is called clinically significant portal hypertension

Above 12 mm Hg, variceal haemorrhaging may occur
