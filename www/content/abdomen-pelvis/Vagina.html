Vagina

Etymology

From Latin, literally ‘sheath, scabbard’.

The muscular tube leading from the external genitals to the cervix of the uterus

Extent and location

Extent - between the vestibule of the vulva and upto the cervix of uterus

Location - between bladder (anterior) and rectum (posterior)

Profile

Profile is H shaped

Each angle is attached to white fascial sheath (arcus tendineus fasciae pelvis)

Walls

Anterior wall

Cervix projects at its upper part

Length 7 cm

Posterior wall

9-10 cm - longer

Orientation of vagina

In the normal standing position,

Bladder, upper two third of vagina and rectum lie in the same horizontal level

Vaginal supports

Are mainly by

Arcus tendineus fasciae pelvis

Cardinal ligament / uterosacral ligament complex

Levator ani

Perineal body

Arterial supply

Upper part of vagina - branches of uterine artery

Middle part of vagina - branches of vaginal artery and branches of middle rectal arteries

Branches vaginal artery and middle rectal artery forms longitudinal arterial anastomoses on the anterior and posterior vaginal wall - called azygos arteries of vagina

Lower part of vagina - branches of vaginal artery and branches from bulb of vestibule

Venous drainage

Vaginal venous plexus

Uterine veins

Vesicular veins

All veins drain into internal iliac veins

Lymphatics

Upper part - external and internal iliac lymph nodes

Middle part - internal iliac nodes

Lower part - sacral and common iliac nodes

Innervation

Uterovaginal plexus - derived from inferior hypogastric plexus

Sensory innervation - (only distal part) by branches of pudendal nerve

Epithelium

​​​​​​​Mucosa is lined with stratified squamous epithelium

Vaginal fornix

Recesses formed due to vaginal part of the cervix

Anterior fornix - Related to vesico-uterine pouch

two lateral fornices - smaller recesses

Posterior fornix - larger recess related to recto-uterine pouch

Pap test (Papanicolaou test)

Screening method to detect the cancerous and precancerous conditions of the uterine cervix

Ayre spatula is used to collect the cells during the pap smear

Broad end - collects vaginal cells

Narrow end - collects cervical cells
