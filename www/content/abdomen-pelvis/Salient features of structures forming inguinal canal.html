Salient features of structures forming inguinal canal

Inguinal ligament

  • Thick in rolled lower border of aponeurosis of external oblique
  • Extend from – ASIS to pubic tubercle
  • Forms – floor of inguinal canal
  • Convexity towards thigh
  • Continuous with fascia lata
  • Length – 12 to 14 cm (adults)
  • Inclined at 35 – 400
  • Medial end of inguinal ligament
    • Expansion posterior and lateral to pecten pubis
    • Lacunar ligament
    • Upwards and medially to rectus sheath and linea alba
    • Reflected part of inguinal ligament

Lacunar ligament

  • = Gimbernat ligament
  • Pectineal part of inguinal ligament

Pectineal ligament (of Astley Cooper)

  • Extends laterally from its base along pecten pubis
  • Augmented by pectineal fascia and by a lateral expansion from the lower end of linea alba
  • Abdiminiculum linea albae

Reflected part of inguinal ligament

  • Expansion from lateral crus of superficial inguinal ring
  • Passes upwards and medially behind the medial end of superficial inguinal ring
  • Fibres of right and left ligaments decussate in linea alba

Superficial inguinal ring

  • Triangular opening
  • Aponeurosis of external oblique
  • Over pubic crest

Superficial inguinal ring is triangular opening in external oblique aponeurosis

Legs of superficial inguinal ring

  • Lateral crus
    • Stronger
    • Formed by fibres of inguinal ligament inserted into the pubic tubercle
  • Medial crus
    • Thin, flat band
    • Attached to front of pubic symphysis
    • Interlaces with opposite crus

External spermatic fascia

  • Delicate tubular prolongation of fibrous tissue around the spermatic cord and testis

Obliquus internus abdominis

  • Lowest fibres become tendinous
  • Attach to corresponding part of the aponeurosis of transversus abdominis
  • Attach to crest and medial part of pectin pubis
  • Forms falx inguinalis – conjoint tendon

Cremaster

  • Loosely arranged muscle fasciculi
  • Lying along the spermatic cord
  • United by areolar tissue to form the sac – like cremastric fascia
  • Around the spermatic cord and testis
  • Within external spermatic fascia
  • Fibres – also attached to internal oblique and transversus abdominis

Cremastric reflex

Transversus abdominis

  • Aponeurosis of lower fibres curve downwards and medially
  • aponeurosis of internal oblique
  • Forms falx inguinalis

Deep inguinal ring is V-shaped defect at the transversalis fascia

Deep inguinal ring is lateral to inferior epigastric artery

Internal spermatic fascia

  • Continuation of fascia transversalis along spermatic cord

Fascia transversalis

  • Entire connective tissue sheet lining the musculature of the abdominal cavity
  • Bilaminar in inguinal area
  • Continues as anterior femoral sheath

Falx inguinalis

  • Conjoint tendon
  • Internal oblique + transverse abdominis
  • Inserts into crest and pectin of pubis
  • Protect the canal from behind
  • Medially – fuses with anterior wall of sheath of rectus abdominis
  • Laterally – blend with interfoveolar ligament

Hesselbach’s triangle

  • Inferior epigastirc artery
  • Lateral border of rectus abdominis
  • Inguinal ligament
  • Floor – transversalis fascia

Most direct inguinal hernias occur in this area

Iliopubic tract

  • Thickened inferior margin of fascia transversalis
  • Fibrous band rinning parallel to inguinal ligament
