Uterine tube

Synonyms

Fallopian tube and salphinx (meaning tubes)

Length

7 to 14 cm

Parts

  • Infundibulum
  • Ampulla
  • Isthmus
  • Intramural part

Arterial supply

Two sources

  • Uterine artery
  • Ovarian artery - supplies ovarian end of the uterine tube

Veins

Run along the arteries

Lymphatics

Drain into the preaortic and aortic lymph nodes

Innervation

Autonomic supply - ovarian and inferior hypogastric plexuses

Pain sensation - relayed in T11, T12, L1, and L2 segments

Clinical importance - tubal pregnancy

Implantation of the conceptus in the uterine tube - tubal pregnancy

Most commonly attaches to the ampulla part

More prone to rupture

Ruptured ectopic - must be considered in all acute abdomen cases among females

Clinical importance – tubectomy

Permanent method of contraception in women

A Falope ring is placed in the isthmic region of the uterine tube
