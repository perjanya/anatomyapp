Jejunum and ileum

Extends from duodenum to the ileocecal junction

Length

Jejunum ~ 2.5 meters

Ileum ~ 3 meters

Suspended in the abdomen through the mesetery

Blood supply

Arterial supply

By jejunal and ileal branches of superior mesenteric artery

Venous drainage

By corresponding veins drain to superior mesenteric vein (portal vein)

Lymphatic drainage

Drain to nodes along superior mesenteric artery and vein

Later the lymph enters cisterna chyli to drain into thoracic duct 

Lymphatics of small intestine carries major portion absorbed lipids 

It can also spread cancer from intestines

Innervation

Parasympathetic supply by vagus nerve

Secretion of glands and motility

Sympathetic supply - splanchnic nerves 

Controls blood supply to the gut, also influences motility and glandular secretions

Functions

Jejunum

Absorb sugars, amino acids and fatty acids

Ileum

Absorbs particularly absorbs Vitamin B12 and bile acids

Also absorbs sugars, amino acids and fatty acids

Difference between jejunum and ileum
