Deep perineal pouch and urogenital diaphragm

Deep perineal pouch

Location

It is an interfascial space across the pubic arch & above the perineal membrane.

It is closed on all sides.

Contributes to the formation of pelvic floor.

Boundaries of deep perineal pouch

Above: superior fascia of urogenital diaphragm.

Below:perineal membrane.

In front: transverse perineal ligament.

Behind: fusion of upper & lower boundaries.

On each side: ischiopubic rami.

Contents – muscles

  • Sphincter urethrae
  • Deep transverse perenei
  • Compressor urethrae(F)
  • Sphincter urethrovaginalis(F) (muscular branches of perineal nerve)

Contents – vessels

Internal pudendal artery passes on each side along pubic arch & divides into 3 terminal branches.

  • Deep artery of penis/clitoris.
  • Dorsal artery of penis/clitoris.
  • Artery to the bulb of penis/vestibule.

Contents – nerves

  • Dorsal nerve of penis / clitoris.
  • Muscular branches from perineal nerve.

Other contents

Membranous urethra

Vagina (F)

Bulbourethral glands (M)

Urogenital diaphragm

It is a Musculo-fascial partition across the pubic arch separating the pelvic cavity from the anterior part of the pelvic outlet.

Consists of 2 muscles:

  • Sphincter urethrae &
  • Deep transverse perinei

Both the muscles are supplied by muscular branches of perineal nerve.

Urogenital diaphragm – relations

Superiorly:

  • Anterior fibres of both levator ani muscles.
  • Anterior recess of ischorectal fossa.
  • apex of prostate (M).
  • Neck of bladder(F).

Inferiorly:

  • Contents of superficial perineal pouch.

Anteriorly:

  • Transverse perineal ligament.

Posteriorly:

  • Ischiorectal fossa.

Actions of urogenital diaphragm

  • It supports the prostate & bladder.
  • It constricts the vagina (F).
  • It fixes the perineal body.
  • Sphincter urethrae muscle exerts voluntary control of micturition & expels the last drops of urine after bladder stops contracting.
