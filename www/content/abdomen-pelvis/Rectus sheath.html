Rectus sheath is formed from decussating fibres from all three lateral abdominal muscles

  • External oblique, internal oblique and transversus abdominis each forms a bilaminar aponeurosis at their medial borders
  • The fibres from all three anterior leaves run obliquely upwards, whereas the posterior leaves run obliquely downwards at right angles to the anterior leaves.

The bilaminar nature of each muscular aponeurosis is difficult to illustrate in cross-section.

The fibres appear to fuse into a single sheet during formation of the rectus sheath.

Applied aspects:

During abdominal incision, decussating fibres maybe used to identify the midline during surgical incisions.

During parasagittal abdominal incision, anterior rectus sheath in incised longitudinally, rectus muscle is pushed to a side, posterior rectus sheath is incised longitudinally before the extraperitoneal fat and peritoneum is approached. While suturing, anterior and posterior layers are sutured separately.

During repair of direct inguinal hernia, a polypropylene mesh is inserted between the posterior rectus sheath and muscle fibres and anchored in place

During repair of recurrent femoral hernia, polypropylene mesh is sutured over the femoral canal orifice and is anchored inferiorly to iliopectineal ligament and medially to rectus sheath.
