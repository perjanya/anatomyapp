Anorectum

Definitions

Portion of large intestine distal to sigmoid colon - is anorectum

The junction between sigmoid colon and rectum is described variously as follows

[HTML] anorectum.html

[INFO]

Anatomical description

  • Junction opposite to left sacroiliac joint
  • At the level of S3 vertebra
  • Level at which sigmoid mesentery disappears

Surgical description

  • The level at which appendices epiploicea and sacculations of sigmoid colon disappears
  • Level at which superior rectal artery divides into right and left branches

Proctoscopy description

  • Level of anterior angulation with constriction
  • At the level of superior rectal fold

Cadaveric description

  • Transition between mucosal rugae of colon to smooth mucosa of rectum

Anal canal and distal end rectum – definitions

Wide disparity between anatomical and surgical description

Anatomical description

Anal canal is distal to pectinate (dentate line)

Surgical description

Anal canal is distal to insertion to levator ani muscles

Surgical anal canal

Anatomical anal canal + distal 2 cm of rectum above pectinate line

Gross anatomy - Curvatures, relations, valves

Curvatures

Anterio-posterior curvatures

  • Sacral curvature - concave anterior
  • Perineal curvature - convex anterior, due to pull by puborectalis

Side to side curvature

  • Upper right lateral
  • Middle left
  • Lower right curvature

Anatomical parts of rectum and peritoneal reflections

Upper third - covered all three sides by peritoneum

Middle third - anterior peritoneal covering

Lower third - No peritoneal covering

Relations of rectum – posterior

Sacrum and fascia of Waldeyer, coccyx and its muscles, levator ani, median sacral vessels, roots of the sacral nerve plexus

Relations of the rectum - anterior (male)

Male (extraperitoneal)

Anterior: prostate, seminal vesicles, vas deferens, ureters, urinary bladder

Relations of rectum - posterior (females)

Female (extraperitoneal)

Anterior: posterior vaginal wall

Lateral: intestinal loops

Intraperitoneal

Anterior: posterior vaginal wall, upper uterus, uterine (fallopian) tubes, ovaries

Lateral: pelvic wall

Posterior: as in the male

Four transverse mucosal folds - inconsistent

First and third - towards left

[INFO]

Surgical anal canal

Definition - anal canal + 2 cm of distal rectum above the pectinate line

Interior of anal canal

Anal columns of Morgagni

Permanent mucosal folds with underlying parallel bundles of muscularis

Anal valves

Crescent shaped folds connecting lower ends of anal columns

[TIP]Pectinate line (dentate line) is the position of anal valves

Anal sinuses or anal crypts

Pockets formed by the anal valves

Anal ducts

Downward and outward running small ducts from anal sinuses on the posterior rectal wall

Anal papillae

Mucosal projections from the junction of anal valves and the anal columns

Pectinate line, dentate line, Hilton's line and mucocutaneous line

Pectinate line = dentate line

Formed by the margins of anal valves

Hilton's line = Mucocutaneous line

Mucocutaneous junction of stratified squamous and columnar epithelium
