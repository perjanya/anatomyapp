Superficial perineal pouch

Location

Superficial to perineal membrane

Stretches across the pubic arch

Boundaries

Superficial: Colles’ fascia.

Deep: perineal membrane.

On each side: ischiopubic rami.

Posteriorly: closed by the fusion of perineal membrane with colles’ fascia.

Anteriorly: open & continuous with the spaces of scrotum, penis & anterior abdominal wall.

Contents of superficial perineal pouch

Root of the penis made up of 2 corpora cavernosa & 1 corpus spongiosum (traversed by urethra).

Body of clitoris made up of 2 corpora cavernosa.

2 bulbs of vestibule, 1 on each side of vaginal orifice unite near glans clitoris.

Muscles in the superficial perineal pouch

Ischiocavernosus covering the corpora cavernosa of penis/clitoris.

Bulbospongiosus covering the corpus spongiosum/bulb of the vestibule.

Superficial transversus perenei.

Superficial pouch – vessels

2 branches of perineal artery-

  • Posterior scrotal/labial arteries.
  • Transverse perineal arteries.

4 branches from the artery of penis-

  • Artery to the bulb of penis/clitoris.
  • Urethral artery.
  • Dorsal artery of penis.
  • Deep artery of penis.

Superficial pouch – nerves

3 branches from the perineal nerve

  • Posterior scrotal / labial n.
  • Branch to the bulb of penis/vestibule.
  • Muscular branches.

Long perineal n. from posterior cutaneous nerve of thigh.

Superficial pouch - other contents

Male

  • Spongy urethra
  • Ducts of bulbourethral glands

Female

  • Urethra
  • Vagina
  • Greater vestibular glands

Greater vestibular / bartholin’s glands

  • Pea shaped mucous glands.
  • Lie posterior to the bulb of vestibule.
  • Homologuous of bulbourethral glands in men.
  • Ducts open into the vestibule of perineum along the posterolateral margins of vaginal opening.
  • Its secretions lubricate the vagina during intercourse.
