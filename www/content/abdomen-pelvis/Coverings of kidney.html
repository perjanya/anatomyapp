Coverings of kidney

Features

  • A unique arrangement of fascia and fat surrounds kidneys
  • Three layers
    • Fibrous capsule
    • Perinephric fat
    • Renal fascia

Fibrous capsule (true capsule)

Condensation of fibrous tissue of kidney

Perinephric fat (perirenal fat)

  • Accumulation of extraperitoneal fat
  • Immediately outside the renal capsule
  • Completely surrounds the kidney

Renal fascia (fascia of Gerota)

  • Membranous condensation of the extraperitoneal fascia
  • Suprarenal glands are also enclosed in this fascial compartment
  • Anterior layer – fascia of Toldt
  • Posterior layer – fascia of Zuckerkandl

[TIP] The renal fascia must be incised in any surgical approach to kidney

Pararenal fat fills

Pararenal fat cells fills the paravertebral gutter and forms cushion to kidneys posteriorly.
