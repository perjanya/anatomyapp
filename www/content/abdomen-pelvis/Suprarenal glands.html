Suprarenal glands

[INFO]

Suprarenal glands - gross anatomy

Synonyms and etymology

Adrenal glands (Adrenal - adjacent to renal)

Suprarenal glands (suprerenal - above the kidney)

Functions

Medulla - acts as sympathetic ganglion, secretes adrenaline and noradrenaline

Cortex - has three layers

  • Zona glomerulosa - secrete Mineralocorticoids (aldosterone)
  • Zona fasiculata - secrete corticosteroids (cortisol)
  • Zona reticularis - secrete sex steroids (dehydroepiandrosterone, androstenodione)

Location

Antero superior to upper pole of kidneys

In epigastrium

Shape

Right suprarenal gland - wedge shaped

Left suprarenal gland - Semilunar

[INFO]

Change in size

At birth - suprarenal glands are large (about one third the size of kidney)

In adults - the size is 1/30th the size of kidney

Hilum of suprarenal gland

Right suprerenal gland hilum - near anterior border, right suprarenal vein emerges

Left suprarenal gland hilum - near lower part of anterior surface, left suprarenal vein emerges

Suprarenal glands - appreciate the different shapes on right and left sides

Coverings

  1. Deeper - loose areolar tissue with perirenal fat
  2. Outer - Renal fascia (encloses kidney as well, separated by a septum)

Arterial supply

Three arteries

  1. Superior suprarenal artery - branch of inferior phrenic artery
  2. Middle suprarenal artery - branch of abdominal aorta
  3. Inferior suprarenal artery - branch of renal artery

Superior suprarenal artery

Venous drainage

Right suprarenal vein - drains directly into inferior vena cava

It is short and is difficult to ligate

Left suprarenal vein - drains into left renal vein opposite to the drainage of left gonadal vein

Right suprarenal vein

Drains into the inferior vena cava

Left suprarenal vein

Drains into the left renal vein

Opposite to left testicular vein in males

Left varicocele

Left sided varicocele is more common

[CLINICAL]
  1. Left testis is at lower level
  2. Left testicular vein may be compressed by loaded sigmoid colon
  3. Left suprarenal vein carrying sympathomimetic compounds drains opposite to left testicular vein to the left renal vein

Applied aspects - functional abnormalities

Chronic adrenal insufficiency - Addison's disease

Acute adrenal insufficiency - adrenal crisis

Hypersecretion of corticosterone - Cushing syndrome

[WARNING]

Applied aspects – adrenalectomy

Adrenal veins are ligated first in cases of pheochromocytomas. This avoids systemic release of catecholamines through adrenal veins

Anatomical complications during adrenalectomy

[CLINICAL]Vascular injuries - left sided adrenalectomy: injury to inferior mesenteric vein, middle and left colic arteries, superior renal polar arteries. Right sided adrenalectomy: injury to hepatic veins, inferior vena cava and superior renal polar arteries

[CLINICAL]Organ injury - left sided adrenalectomy: splenic injury, pancreatic injury. Right sided adrenalectomy: injury to liver, duodenum
