Seminal vesicles and ejaculatory ducts

Gland and location

Saccular tubular glands located on posterior surface of the base of the urinary bladder

Relations

Anterior and superior: urinary bladder

Posterior and inferior: Denonvillier’s fascia

Medial: ductus deferens

Lateral: levator ani

Below: ejaculatory duct

Length

15 cm when uncoiled

Interesting fact: Seminal vesicles are present only in herbivorous animals. All carnivorous animals do not have seminal vesicles. This is one of the anatomical factors pointing the vegetarian-ness of the humans ALONG WITH

  • Grinding teeth
  • Ptyalin in saliva
  • Very long intestinal tract
  • Small liver
  • Convoluted colon
  • Millions of pores

Corresponding structure in females

Skene glands / paraurethral glands / female prostate

Blood and nerve supply

Arterial supply: from vesiculodeferential artery

Veins: drain into prostatic plexus of veins

Innervation: hypogastric nerve

Ejaculatory duct

Formation

By union of ampulla of ductus deferens and distal part of seminal vesicle

Dimensions

2 cm length and 1 mm diameter

Course and termination

Passes distally through the prostate

End as small opening on either side of verumonatum of urethral ridge

Retrograde ejaculation

  • Smooth muscle of the prostatic urethra prevents the retrograde ejaculation
  • There is no mechanism detailed in the ejaculatory duct preventing retrograde ejaculation.
  • Curvy shape of the ejaculatory duct, aided (possibly by) a fold of mucosa, acting as valve, may be additional factors preventing retrograde ejaculation.
