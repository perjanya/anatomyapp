Pudendal nerve

Pudendal nerve

Etymology and introduction

Latin pudenda, meaning external genitals, derived from pudendum, meaning "parts to be ashamed of"

[INFO]Nerve supplying structures in urogenital and anal triangles and external genitalia

Origin and root value

From sacral plexus

Root value - S2, S3 and S4

Course

  • Intrapelvic part
  • Gluteal part
  • Perineal part

Intrapelvic part

Reach greater sciatic foramen below the piriformis muscle

Gluteal part

Winds around the ischial spine and sacrotuberous ligament

[INFO]Three structures related here are (PIN)

  1. Internal pudendal vessels
  2. Pudendal nerve
  3. Nerve to obturator internus

Perineal part

Begins at the pudendal canal or Alcock’s canal in the lateral wall of ischiorectal fossa

Inferior rectal nerve arises here

In the anterior part of this canal - pudendal nerve divides into two terminal branches

  1. Perineal nerve
  2. Dorsal nerve of the penis / clitoris

Branches of pudendal nerve

Inferior rectal nerve (or hemorrhoidal nerve)  

Supplies the external anal sphincter (motor) and takes sensations from lower one third of the anal canal, perianal skin and lower part of vagina

Pudendal nerve divides into two terminal branches

  1. Perineal nerve
  2. Dorsal nerve of the penis / clitoris

Perineal nerve

Branches

Posterior scrotal nerve or labial nerve - supply posterior 2/3rd of the scrotal skin or labial skin

Muscular branches - supply five muscles, superficial transverse perinei, bulbospongiosus, ischiocavernosus, deep transverse perinei and spincter urethrae

Dorsal perineal nerve of penis or clitoris

Male - Supply penile skin, glans and prepuce

Female - supply the dorsal surface of clitoris

[CLINICAL]

Pudendal Nerve Block = saddle nerve block

Method of choice utilized for diagnosis and management of chronic pelvic pain

Earlier, the pudendal nerve block was used for perineal anesthesia during labour. 

The nerve is infiltrated near the ischial spine by a needle passed through the vaginal wall & then guided by a finger.
