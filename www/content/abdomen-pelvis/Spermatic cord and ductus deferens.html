Spermatic cord and ductus deferens

Definition

Spermatic cord is a matrix of connective tissue continuous proximally with the preperitoneal connective tissue

Extension

From the upper pole of the testis to deep inguinal ring

Coverings of the spermatic cord

Three fasciae

  • External spermatic fascia (from external oblique fascia)
  • Cremasteric fascia (from internal oblique fascia)
  • Internal spermatic fascia (from fascia transversalis)

Components of spermatic cord

​​​​​​​Ductus deferens

  1. Three arteries
  • Testicular artery
  • Cremasteric artery
  • Artery to vas deferens
  1. Three veins
  • Pampiniform plexus and testicular vein
  • Cremasteric vein
  • Veins from vas deferens
  1. Three nerves
  • Genital branch of genitofemoral nerve
  • Ilioinguinal nerve
  • Sympathetic nerves (testicular plexus)
  1. Lymphatics

Ductus deferens

Thick-walled muscular tube extending from tail of epididymis up to joining of duct of seminal vesicle

Length - 45 cms

Structures that are 45 cm (18 inches)

  • Ductus deferens
  • Umbilical cord
  • Spinal cord
  • Thoracic duct
  • Transverse colon
  • Sartorius muscle
  • Distance between the incisor tooth to the cardiac end of the stomach  

Course

Passes through four regions

  • Scrotum

begins as continuation of the epididymis

  • Spermatic cord (within the inguinal canal)

From the upper pole of testis to deep inguinal ring via superficial inguinal ring and inguinal canal

  • Abdominal

Begins at the deep inguinal ring

Turns around the inferior epigastric artery

Crosses the external iliac vessels

Later located within the lateral wall of pelvis

  • Pelvic cavity

In the pelvis - it crosses superior vesical vessels, obturator nerve, obturator vessels, inferior vesical vessels and ureter

Turns towards the base of urinary bladder

Joins the duct of seminal vesicle and forms ejaculatory duct

Amupulla

Terminal dilated part of ductus deferens

Ductus deferens

At risk relations in the pelvis

  • Lateral to the umbilical artery
  • Lateral to the obturator nerve and vessels
  • Lateral to the superior vesical vessels
  • Anteromedial side of the ureter
  • Posterior aspect of the bladder
  • Medial to the seminal vesicles where it becomes dilated as the ampulla

Blood supply

Ductus deferens is supplied by artery of ductus deferens (branch of inferior vesicular artery)

Deferential vein drains into the pelvic plexus

Applied aspects - Torsion of testis

Twisting of spermatic cord

Leading to cutting off the blood supply to testis

Patient suffers from excruciating pain

Cremasteric reflex is absent

Prolonged ischemia may interfere with sperm production

It’s a surgical emergency

Immediate surgical intervention to restore the blood supply can salvage the testis

Applied aspects – Vasectomy

Surgical procedure of where the ductus deferens is cut and ligated for permanent sterilization of the males

Anatomical basis

Ductus deferns tube is cut and ligated - Lead to blockage of the tube carrying semen

During the procedure, ductus is cut high in the scrotum, routinely a part of the ductus is also cut to prevent spontaneous recalization

No-Scalpel Vasectomy (NSV)

A special hemostat is used to make the scrotal skin puncture. 

Ductus deferens is identified and ligated
