Ovary

Definition

Ovaries are female gonad

From modern Latin ovarium, from Latin ovum ‘egg’

Location

In the ovarian fossa of the lateral pelvic wall

In multiparous women, the location varies

Ovarian fossa

Shallow recess in the lateral wall of pelvis

Bounded by

Superiorly by - external iliac vessels and obturator nerve

Anteriorly by - attachment of broad ligament to the lateral pelvic wall

Posteriorly by - internal iliac vessels and ureter

Relations of the ovary

Relations of the ovary

​​​​​​​Relations - lateral and medial relations

Superficial to deep the structures related to lateral wall of the ovary are

  • Parietal peritoneum
  • Obturator nerve and vessels
  • Obturator fascia
  • Obturator internus muscle

Medial surface of the ovary is in contact with fimbrial end of uterine tube

Measurements

  • Almond shaped
  • 1 cm X 2 cm x 3 cm
  • Weigh approximately 4 to 5 grams
  • White in color
  • Right ovary usually larger than left

Features of the ovary

Borders

Anterior border - related to mesovarium

Posterior border - is free

Poles

Upper pole - related to uterine tube, and to infudibulo-pelvic ligament

Lower pole - connected to lateral wall of the uterus by utero-ovarian ligament

Ligaments of ovary

  1. Utero-ovarian ligament (proper ligament of ovary / ovarian ligament)
  2. Mesovarium
  3. Suspensory ligament of ovary (infundibulo-pelvic ligament)

Utero-ovarian ligament (proper ligament of ovary / ovarian ligament)

Cord-like structure made of connective tissue and smooth muscles

Extends from the lower pole of the ovary to the lateral wall of the uterus

Located between the mesovarium and mesosalphix

Gubernaculum of ovary forms ligament of ovary

Mesovarium

Fold of peritoneum suspending the ovary from the posterior and superior aspect of the broad ligament.

Mesovarium do not cover the ovary.

It attaches the ovary the anterior border of the ovary to the posterior aspect of the broad ligament

It carries blood vessels from broad ligament to the ovary

Suspensory ligament of ovary (infundibulo-pelvic ligament)

Is the fold of the peritoneum covering the vessels, nerves and lymphatics to the lateral pelvic wall

Extends from upper pole of the ovary to the lateral pelvic wall via infundibulum of the uterine tube

Arterial supply

Ovarian arteries - branches of abdominal aorta

Supply the ovary and lateral parts of uterine tubes

Anastomose on the lateral aspects of the uterus with uterine arteries

Travel through the infundibulo-pelvic ligament

Venous drainage

Right side - vein drains into the inferior vena cava

Left side - vein drains into the left renal vein

Lymphatics

Lymphatics drain into the lateral aortic (para-aortic) group of lymph nodes

Innervation

Visceral sensory fibres - travel through splanchnic nerves to reach T10, T11 and T12 segments

Visceral sympathetic innervation - aorticorenal plexus - travel via sympathetic nerves to T10 to T12 segments

Ovarian pain is referred to the lower abdomen and pubic region

Parasympathetic fibres (S2, S3, and S4) - by inferior hypogastric plexus via pelvic splanchnic nerves
