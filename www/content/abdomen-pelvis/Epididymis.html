Epididymis

Etymology and definition

  • Etymology - from Greek epididumis, from epi ‘upon’ + didumos ‘testicle’ (from duo ‘two’)
  • Epididymis - Long, highly coiled tube attached to posterior border of testis
  • Connects - testis and vas deferens

Length in adults

6 -7 meters

Location

Posterolateral to testis

On the lateral side - Sinus of epididymis - recess between the body of the epididymis and the testis

This helps in side determination of isolated testis.

This is the site of entry of blood vessels and nerves to the testis and epididymis

Parts

  • Head - related to superior pole of testis
  • Body - related to posterior border
  • Tail - continues as ductus deferens

Microanatomy and functions

The epithelium is pseudostratified, with long immotile 'sterocilia' which are in fact very long microvilli.

These are thought to be involved in absorption of fluid.

The cells are also thought to be secretory, but the nature of the secretions is unknown
