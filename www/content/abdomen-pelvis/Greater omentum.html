Greater omentum

Fold of peritoneum attached to stomach on its greater curvature

Milky spots

Aggregates of lymphocytes

Policemen of abdomen

Greater Omentum - “Abdominal policeman" of the peritoneal cavity

Arterial supply of greater omentum

By Right and left gastroepiploic arteries

and by Branches of Pancreatic Artery

Omentopexy

A surgical procedure where greater oemntum is used to

  • Fill the gap or defect (as in peptic ulcer perforation)
  • To supplement arterial supply (as in thromboangitis obliterans) 
  • To enhance lymphatic drainage
  • To absorb effusions

Embryological basis

Most of the ventral mesentery disappears – except ventral mesogastrium

Fold of peritoneum between anterior abdominal wall and liver forms – falciform ligament

Fold of peritoneum between liver and stomach forms lesser omentum

Fold of dorsal mesogastrium caudal to stomach forms greater omentum

Fold of dorsal mesogastrium between spleen and stomach forms gastroslenic ligament transmitting short gastric arteries

Fold of dorsal mesogastrium between spleen and kidney forms lienorenal ligament and transmits splenic artery
