Peculiarities of Clavicle

The clavicle is the only long bone in the body that lies horizontally. It has several unique characteristics that distinguish it from other bones.

  • It is the first bone to ossify in the body, beginning in the 5th week of fetal development
  • It is the last bone to complete ossification, around age 25 years
  • It has no medullary cavity, unlike typical long bones
  • It is subcutaneous throughout its entire length, making it easily palpable
  • It is the most commonly fractured bone in the human body

The clavicle serves as a strut to keep the upper limb away from the thorax, allowing for maximum freedom of movement. It transmits forces from the upper limb to the axial skeleton.
