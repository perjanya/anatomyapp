Lymphatic Drainage of the Breast

The breast has an extensive lymphatic drainage system. Most lymph from the breast drains to the axillary lymph nodes.

Axillary Lymph Nodes

The axillary lymph nodes are divided into several groups based on their anatomical location.

Groups of Axillary Nodes

  • Pectoral (anterior) nodes: Located along the lateral thoracic vessels
  • Subscapular (posterior) nodes: Along the subscapular vessels
  • Lateral (humeral) nodes: Along the axillary vein
  • Central nodes: Located in the center of the axilla
  • Apical nodes: At the apex of the axilla

Clinical Importance

Breast Cancer Metastasis

Understanding the lymphatic drainage of the breast is crucial for breast cancer staging and treatment. Axillary lymph node involvement is a key prognostic factor.
